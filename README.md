# Music and Mental Health Data Analysis
Analyzing the relationship between Music and Mental Health.

## Questions
- Are types of mental health issues and the choice of genre associated?
- Is there a relationship between hours spent listening and reported mental health benefits? Is more listening always better, or is there a point of diminishing returns?
- Do people who say music improves their mental health actually have higher baseline in anxiety and depression? 

## Findings
- There are no major differences between mental health diagnoses and the choice of genre. 
- There is a positive relationship between hours spent listening to music and reported mental health benefits. Meaning, the more hours one listens to music, the more likely they are to say music has improved their mental health. However, more hours listening to music does not always mean better; after 3-5 hours there is a decline in percieved mental health benefit.
- Those who say music improves their mental health tend to have a higher baseline in anxiety and depression.

## Data Source
[Kaggle - Music & Mental Health Survey Results] (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/catherinerasgaitis/mxmh-survey-results)
